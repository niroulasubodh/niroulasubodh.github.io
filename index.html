About Me

My passion for bioinformatics began during my first year at Soka University of America, when I created a simple Python program to convert RNA sequences to protein sequences. This initial spark grew into a sustained passion as I continued to find opportunities to integrate computation across scientific disciplines.

From analyzing glucose sensor data at Equilibrio startup to investigating cancer metastasis at UCI Health, my journey has been driven by curiosity and a commitment to using computational approaches to advance biomedical research.

Currently, I'm conducting comparative transcriptomic analysis of UHRF1's role in osteosarcoma and small-cell lung cancer for my senior thesis at Soka University of America.
